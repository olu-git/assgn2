A Window of Opportunity in the Housing Market?

Housing prices have been soaring globally, especially over the past decade. While certain demographics benefit from this, the majority face the daunting ramifications of a heated property market.

Below is exploration of how housing prices have changed in recent times. Specifically, the global and Australian housing market will be briefly inspected before more detailed insight is provided on Melbourne's market.

Various measures are used such as the House Price Index (HPI) of a few indices, average pricing and median pricing. These measures allow for meaningful comparison between nations, states, and even suburbs.


Indices Rapidly Rose in the 2010's

Note - Missing data because of lacking collection tools relate to a nation's economic weakness.

Real Prices: Nominal HPI to the consumer's expenditure deflator that compares pricing between each country.

Rent Prices: Price changes for housing rentals.

Price to Income: A measure of affordability that divides the nominal HPI by the nominal disposable income per person.

Price to Rent: A measure of profitability of house ownership that divdes the nominal HPI housing rent price index.

"Real Prices" indicate that nations like China and the US currently have a overheated housing market, while Australia's is not far behind - being slightly more stable.


The Australian Housing Market is Booming

Here, we have the median house pricing in Australian capital cities and Australian states from 2004 to 2021.

In terms of States, it is evident that New South Wales consisently remains the costliest for living followed closely by Victoria. A similar trend is seen with the capital cities too.

We can see that Sydney tends to have the ghest median house pricinghi followed by Melbourne. Meanwhile, mainland Australia's east cost seems to have the highest overall pricing, possibly due to greater level of population.

Overall, there has been an increasing trend in housing prices across all of Australia, especially during the past few years due to government and RBA policy.


Melbourne's Inner Suburbs are the Most Expensive

Immediately, it is obvious that outer Melbourne suburbs seem to have less monthly flucations than those near the CBD. However, Riddle's Creek and Frankston South seem to anomalies given their distance from the city.

The CBD area seems to be the most expensive. This is likely due to its centrality and proximity to jobs and important locations. In addition, Melbourne's heritage and general city aspect add to each suburbs housing valuation.

Urban sprawl from the city becomes apparent as the Southern Metropolitan area appears to be the next most expensive Suburban area. As this region is more populated, the housing market here is more expensive.

By zooming in and changing the map centre, more detail is shown for each of the suburbs in Melbourne. Statewide comparison become possible as well as immediate sububr comparisons.


A Peek into Melbourne's Housing Situation

A Large Proportion of Houses Cost Over $1M

To no surprise, a large amount of houses are worth over $1 million in Melbourne. The distribution of the prices is heavily skewed to the right - making the mean greater than the median.

By selecting specific bin regions, the median and mean line change to fit that region. Although it is unnoticeable, there are a few houses ranging from $5M-$9M.



New Homeowners should Buy in West Melbourne

The Southern Metropolitan is the most expensive area but also contains the widest spread of house prices - suggesting high levels of inequality.

Meanwhile, the developing Western Metropolitan area is the cheapest region and could serve as an entry point for first-home buyers. As an outlier, Boroondara is a council area of interest that will be explored later in the bubble plot below.




Units are Not Always Cheap

By looking at the top 3 most expensive suburbs in each region, it is no surprise that houses account for most of the priciest properties. However, units tend to be the second most priciest property type in the populated and expensive regions of South-East Melbourne.

As a property buyer, townhouses may be an optimal choice if choosing to live in an affluent suburb.

Older Homes could be a Winning Formula

This heatmap indicates the correlation between important variables pertaining to Melbourne properties. The higher the correlation (R) value, the stronger the relationship.

Older houses may be cheaper, as indicated by a negative, albeit weak R-value - thus heritage properties are not in high demand. The relationship between rooms and prices becomes important in the next section.


The Bigger the House, the Larger the Fee

In the boxplot above, the council area of Boroondara was noted because it served as three outliers in the Southern Metropolitan. Thereby, Boroondara was selected as the area of choice for this bubble plot where we look at the relationship between price and landsize - adding property type and rooms as extra dimensions.

As landsize increases, so do property prices - exemplifying the moderately positive relationship between the two variables. In addition, room numbers rise with house pricing which furthers the relationship shown between the two variables shown in the heatmap. As the minimum price rises, units begin to vanish and then townhouses. This reflects the fact that the two aforementioned property types are far cheaper than houses. Budget buyers may need to consider less rooms and having units or townhouses as their home of choice.

It is worth noting, that one unit in Camberwell is far more expensive than other units, honing four rooms and being valued at nearly $2.6M.


House Pricing in Expensive Suburbs is Widening

The line chart here highlights how house prices over the past 10 years have changed across the top five costliest suburbs in Melbourne.

Brighton and Canterbury have consistently shared the title of the most expensive suburb in the past decade until property values surged in the past year for Middle Park.

Meanwhile, the bar chart displays the average price of each suburb for the selected period of time.

Here, we reaffirm that Brighton and Canterbury indeed have the highest property valuation of the period - valued at over $2 million.

The ability to select specific years and find the average house price allows for better overall comparison of Suburb affordability. On the other hand, wealthy individuals can determine the most prestige suburbs to live in.


To conclude, property prices tend to be higher as we approach the CBD. More specifically, the Southern Metropolitan area has the most expensive properties, the Boroondora council area contain affluent suburbs, and Canterbury is the costliest suburbs with house prices averaging over $2.3 million. Home buyers should consider several aspects before purchasing their homes such as landsize, garage space and especially the number of rooms in their house. Alternatively, developing regions like the Western Metropolitan could be ideal for both new families and investors.
